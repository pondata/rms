Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions from business owners exploring IT services.

How much does managed IT support cost for a small business?

Managed IT pricing typically ranges from $100 to $250 per user per month depending on the level of support, number of devices, and services included. We offer flat-rate plans so you can budget predictably without surprise invoices. Contact us for a custom quote based on your specific needs.

What is the difference between break-fix and managed IT services?

Break-fix means you call us when something breaks and pay per incident. Managed IT is a proactive model where we monitor, maintain, and secure your systems continuously for a fixed monthly fee — preventing most issues before they cause downtime. For most businesses, managed IT is more cost-effective and less disruptive.

How quickly can you respond when we have an IT emergency?

Our average response time is under 15 minutes for critical issues. We provide 24/7 monitoring and tiered response times based on severity — critical outages get immediate attention, while routine requests are handled within a few hours.

Can you help us migrate to Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace?

Yes. We handle full cloud migrations including email, file storage, and collaboration tools. We plan the migration to minimize disruption, handle data transfer, configure security settings, and train your team on the new platform.

Do we really need cybersecurity if we're a small business?

Absolutely. Over 40% of cyberattacks target small businesses, and the average cost of a data breach for a small company can exceed $100,000. We implement layered security including endpoint protection, email filtering, and employee awareness training to protect your business.

What happens to our data if a server fails or we get hit with ransomware?

With our backup and disaster recovery service, your data is backed up automatically to secure offsite locations. If a failure or attack occurs, we can restore your systems quickly — often within hours rather than days.

Can you work with our existing IT person or internal team?

Yes. Many of our clients have an internal IT contact and use us for specialized projects, overflow support, or after-hours coverage. We integrate seamlessly with your existing team rather than replacing them.
