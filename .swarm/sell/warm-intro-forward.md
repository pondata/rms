# Warm-intro forward — what to send to friends + family

The audit's data says **warm intros from someone the prospect trusts** beat every other channel for an MSP. A 5-line note to 10 friends asking for an intro is worth more than 100 cold emails.

---

## Email/text to friends (BCC, but personalize the lead)

**Subject:** Quick favor — I just launched RMS

> Hey [FIRST_NAME],
>
> Just launched River Mountain Systems — managed IT for Vancouver-area businesses. $99/user/month, posted price, 30-day pilot, all the things big-MSPs make hard.
>
> One favor: **do you know anyone running a 10–100-person business in Clark County or Portland metro who's frustrated with their current IT?** If so, an intro would mean the world. Easy version below — feel free to copy/paste.
>
> Either way, thanks for being part of this. Means a lot.
>
> Justin
> (360) 644-4820 · rivermountainsystems.com
>
> ---
>
> **Easy intro template you can forward:**
>
> "Hey [contact] — wanted to introduce you to my friend Justin Oberg. He just launched a managed IT firm out of Vancouver focused on businesses in our size range, and he's actually transparent about pricing and SLAs which I haven't seen anywhere else. Worth a 30-min call if you're not happy with your current setup. He's at (360) 644-4820 / rivermountainsystems.com / joberg@rivermountainsystems.com."

---

## Why this format works

- **You're not asking for the intro** in the first paragraph — you're updating them on news, then asking
- **The forwardable template** removes the "I don't know what to say" friction. Most warm intros die because the friend doesn't want to write the intro email themselves.
- **No ask for review or social share** — just an intro. One ask per email.
- **The "easy template"** is the actual ask. People will copy-paste it verbatim.

## Who to send to

In rough order of likely-to-actually-help:

1. **Past coworkers** in any operational/IT role at a company you respect — they know small businesses and they know your work
2. **Past clients from your prior consulting work** (Total Transit, Marengo, Load to Ride — overlap with proof-asset asks; these are different requests so OK to send both)
3. **Family members who run businesses themselves** — they may be the prospect, or they know prospects
4. **Business-owner friends in any unrelated industry** — they know other business owners
5. **Old college / industry / local-meetup friends** — surprisingly common source of intros
6. **Your accountant, lawyer, banker, insurance agent** — these people know everyone and benefit when their clients work with each other
7. **People you've helped in the last 5 years** — favor reciprocity is real

Aim for 10 messages. Sent personally, not bulk. Should take 30 minutes.

---

## What to expect

Realistic: of 10 friends, **3 will reply, 1 will actually intro**. That 1 intro becomes a 30-min call, which becomes a 1-in-3 close (warm leads close at ~30–40% for a 0-review MSP). So:

- 10 friends → 1 likely intro
- 1 intro → 1 warm conversation
- 1 warm conversation → ~30% chance of a paid pilot

Numerically: **30 friends asked = 1 paid pilot (likely a small project, $500–$3K).** The math gets better with proof — once you have that first Google review and logo, the close rate roughly doubles.

---

## What NOT to ask for in this email

- Don't ask them to share on LinkedIn (separate ask, separate post — see linkedin-post.md)
- Don't ask for testimonials (they're not clients yet)
- Don't ask them to retain you for their own IT (different relationship)
- Don't tell the whole pitch — that's the prospect's job to learn from the website
