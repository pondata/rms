# 1. Greater Vancouver Chamber of Commerce

- **URL (membership):** https://www.vancouverusa.com/membership/join-the-chamber/
- **Member directory (post-join):** https://members.vancouverusa.com/list
- **Cost (Y1 typical):** $300–$600 depending on tier (Solo / Small Business / Premier). Confirm exact tier on the join page before submitting.
- **What it gets you:** member directory listing with backlink, eligibility for Chamber events, "Member of GVCC" trust signal, intro list of other Clark County businesses (warm referral pipeline), discounted ad rates in Chamber publications.
- **How to apply:**
  1. Go to the membership page above.
  2. Pick a tier (start at the lowest — usually called "Solo" or "Small Business").
  3. Fill the application: business name, address, phone, website, owner, # employees (1), industry (Technology / IT Services), description (use ≤500-char version above).
  4. Pay dues (credit card or invoice).
  5. Wait for member-services rep to email and schedule onboarding.
- **Expected approval:** 3–7 business days.
- **HARD GATE:** **paid — Justin only.** Do not submit until Justin has approved the tier and the spend.
- **Required from Justin:** business address, EIN (sometimes asked), credit card.

# 2. Clark County Business Listings (3 sub-targets)

Three overlapping orgs serve Clark County WA. Hit all three — they cross-reference and the listings are free or cheap.

### 2a. Identity Clark County

- **URL:** https://www.identityclarkcounty.org/
- **Member directory:** https://www.identityclarkcounty.org/members/
- **Cost:** membership tiered; **investor-tier** is paid (multi-thousand). Free option: appear on regional partner pages if you're listed in CREDC.
- **Action:** skip direct membership in Y1. Use CREDC pathway (2c) to get incidental exposure.

### 2b. Clark County (WA) — Business Resources

- **URL:** https://clark.wa.gov/community-development/business-resources
- **Cost:** free (it's the county government, not a directory per se).
- **Action:** make sure your WA Secretary of State business registration (UBI) is current. Link from your homepage to your WA SOS listing — county pages aggregate from there.

### 2c. Columbia River Economic Development Council (CREDC)

- **URL:** https://www.credc.org/
- **Investor / member page:** https://www.credc.org/about/investors/
- **Cost:** free for basic regional-business inclusion via the SW WA business database; paid for "Investor" status.
- **Action:** email info@credc.org with NAP + 500-char description and ask to be added to the SW Washington business resource list. Free, low effort, decent backlink.
- **Expected approval:** 1–3 weeks.

# 3. Better Business Bureau (BBB) — Pacific Northwest

- **Listing search (start here):** https://www.bbb.org/us/wa/vancouver
- **Accreditation application:** https://www.bbb.org/get-accredited
- **Two paths:**
  - **Basic listing — FREE:** any registered business can be listed. BBB sometimes auto-creates a profile from public records. Search your business first; if it exists, claim it. If not, request a listing via the BBB site.
  - **Accreditation — PAID:** ~$500/yr for solo / small (BBB scales by employee count). Gets you the BBB Accredited logo, A+ rating eligibility, dispute-resolution flow.
- **Required for accreditation:**
  - EIN
  - WA business license / UBI
  - Certificate of insurance (general liability — confirms you're insured)
  - 1+ year in business (usually — they'll review case-by-case)
  - Clean complaint record
- **Expected review:** accreditation 7–14 days after application.
- **Recommendation:** **claim free listing now; defer accreditation to Y2.** SEO benefit of the free listing is ~80% of the accredited listing. Accreditation logo helps conversion on the website but isn't worth $500 until you have proof assets (case studies, testimonials) that the logo amplifies.
- **HARD GATE:** accreditation = paid. Justin only.
