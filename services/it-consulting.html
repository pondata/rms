Technology Roadmaps & Strategic Planning

Most small businesses make technology decisions reactively — buying what a vendor recommends, replacing what breaks, or adopting what a competitor uses. We take a different approach: understanding your business goals first, then mapping the technology investments that support them.

  • Current-state assessment of your entire technology stack — hardware ages, software versions, contract terms, and pain points
  • 3-year technology roadmap that sequences upgrades, migrations, and new deployments by business priority
  • Capital vs. operational expenditure planning — when to buy, when to lease, when to subscribe
  • Alignment with business milestones — new office openings, headcount growth, or new service launches
  • Annual roadmap reviews that adjust the plan as your business and the technology landscape evolve
3yr

Planning horizon

Technology decisions made without a plan tend to create technical debt. A 3-year roadmap gives you enough visibility to make smart investments today while keeping flexibility for future changes. We update it annually so it stays relevant.

Vendor Evaluation & Management

Choosing between competing software platforms, ISPs, or hardware vendors is time-consuming and high-stakes. We bring vendor-neutral expertise to every evaluation — no commissions, no partnerships that bias our recommendations. Our only interest is what works best for your business.

  • Requirements gathering and RFP development for major technology purchases
  • Side-by-side vendor comparisons based on your specific use case, not generic feature matrices
  • Contract review and negotiation — identifying unfavorable terms, hidden costs, and lock-in clauses
  • Implementation oversight to ensure vendors deliver what they promised
  • Ongoing vendor relationship management — escalation paths, SLA tracking, and renewal negotiations
$0

Vendor commissions we accept

We do not take referral fees or commissions from technology vendors. When we recommend a platform or provider, it is because we believe it is the best fit for your business — full stop. This independence is fundamental to how we operate.

IT Budgeting & Cost Optimization

Technology spending should be predictable and defensible. We help you build IT budgets that account for everything — not just the obvious costs like hardware and software, but the hidden ones like training, integration, and end-of-life replacements.

  • Complete IT spend analysis covering hardware, software, services, telecom, and internal labor
  • License audit to identify unused subscriptions, duplicate tools, and tier-optimization opportunities
  • Total cost of ownership modeling for major technology decisions — buy vs. build vs. subscribe
  • Annual IT budget templates with lifecycle replacement schedules built in
  • Benchmarking your IT spend against industry averages for businesses of your size and sector
15-25%

Typical savings found in first audit

Most businesses have software licenses nobody uses, overlapping tools that do the same thing, and contracts that auto-renewed at higher rates. A thorough IT spend analysis almost always finds meaningful savings — often enough to fund the improvements you actually need.

Compliance & Risk Guidance

If your business handles sensitive data — customer financials, health records, payment card information, or employee PII — you likely have compliance obligations that affect your technology choices. We help you understand what applies to you and build systems that meet those requirements.

  • Compliance framework identification — HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOC 2, CMMC, state privacy laws
  • Gap analysis comparing your current controls to framework requirements
  • Remediation planning with prioritized, actionable steps
  • Policy development — acceptable use, data handling, incident response, and access control policies
  • Audit preparation support and documentation for compliance assessments
?

Do you know your compliance obligations?

Many small businesses are subject to data protection regulations without realizing it. If you accept credit cards, you need PCI compliance. If you handle health data for clients, HIPAA applies. We help you identify what matters and build a realistic path to compliance.
