Common questions

What does managed IT actually include?

Helpdesk support, 24/7 endpoint monitoring, automated patching, antivirus and EDR, vendor coordination (ISP, SaaS), M365 or Google Workspace administration, and quarterly business reviews. Higher tiers add advanced cybersecurity, daily backups, and dedicated engineering time. See full breakdown →

What's the difference between break-fix and managed IT?

Break-fix means you call when something breaks and pay per incident — typically $150–$200/hr. Managed IT is a flat monthly fee that covers monitoring, patching, helpdesk, and most issues. The math usually favors managed IT once you're past about 8 employees.

How fast do you respond?

Critical issues (server down, ransomware, can't work): under 15 minutes during business hours. Standard requests: within 2 hours. Tier 3 plans include 24/7 critical response with after-hours pager.

Do you do work onsite?

Yes. Most issues are resolved remotely, but onsite visits are included for hardware deployment, office moves, and anything that genuinely requires hands-on work. We cover Vancouver, WA and the Portland metro within a 30-mile radius.

Can you work alongside our internal IT person?

Yes — we co-manage with internal IT teams regularly. Common splits: we handle infrastructure, security, and after-hours; internal IT handles user-facing tickets and business systems.

What if I just want a one-time project, not a retainer?

That's fine. We do fixed-scope projects (cloud migrations, network deployments, security remediation, backup setup) standalone. See project starting prices →

Do you offer cybersecurity-only engagements?

Yes — see the cybersecurity remediation sprint on our pricing page (from $4,500). Or buy the IT Health Check ($497) for an audit-only engagement.
