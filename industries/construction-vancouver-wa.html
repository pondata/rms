What we hear from GCs and subs

The five problems that keep showing up

Construction IT problems are not generic office IT problems. The job lives outside the office, the schedule does not forgive a half-day outage, and the threat surface is wider than most owners realize. These are the patterns we see again and again across Vancouver, WA construction firms.

  • Jobsite Wi-Fi blackouts. A site router goes down at 7:30 a.m. Foremen cannot pull plans, daily logs do not sync, and the crew stands around. A single bad day on a remote site can cost $5,000 or more in idle labor and rework — and the carrier ticket queue does not care that you are framing today.
  • File-version chaos. Three superintendents email the same plan revision, the office uploads a fourth, and now the field is building from RFI #14 while the architect is on RFI #17. The fix is not "more SharePoint training." The fix is one source of truth wired into Procore and enforced.
  • Ransomware via "submittal" PDFs. Construction inboxes get hammered with attachments from people the team has never met — vendors, inspectors, owners' reps. Attackers know this and they exploit it. We see fake submittals, fake change orders, and fake lien-waiver PDFs carrying loaders that pivot to your accounting server.
  • Mobile-device loss on jobsites. Tablets walk off. Phones get crushed. Laptops go in trucks that get broken into. Without MDM, every lost device is a credential-leak event and a billable replacement at full retail.
  • Accounts-payable phishing. A "vendor" emails new ACH instructions. AP pays. By the time the real vendor calls about the missing check, the wire is gone. This is the single most expensive incident pattern we see in construction — typical loss is $40K to $250K, and it almost always lands on a firm with no dual-control payment process.

Sample stack

What a healthy construction IT stack looks like

This is the configuration we recommend and deploy for most 8–40 person Vancouver, WA construction firms. It is not the only valid stack — but it is the one that holds up under jobsite reality, audit pressure, and the scale curve from $5M to $50M annual revenue.

Project management
Procore as the single source of truth for drawings, RFIs, submittals, daily logs, and punch lists. We handle implementation, user provisioning, integration with accounting, and field rollout.
Accounting
Sage 100 Contractor or Sage 300 CRE, properly licensed, properly backed up, integrated with Procore for budget actuals. We do not replace your bookkeeper — we make sure the system they run on does not lose data.
File storage
On-prem fileserver migration to Microsoft 365 / SharePoint with field-friendly permissions. The "G: drive" goes away. The plans live in one place. Old files get archived, not deleted.
Jobsite Wi-Fi
Mesh access points sized for the site, LTE/5G failover so a downed coax line does not stop work, weatherized enclosures where needed, and a defined process for setup and teardown that a foreman can run without a ticket.
Ruggedized devices
Foreman tablets that survive a job trailer. MDM on every device so a lost tablet is a five-minute remote-wipe instead of a credential incident.
PDF markup
Bluebeam Revu deployed and licensed correctly across estimating, project management, and the field. Templates and tool sets standardized so markups read the same on every drawing.
Endpoint security
Bitdefender or Sophos on every laptop and server. Email filtering tuned for the construction inbox profile (lots of unknown senders with attachments). MFA on M365, Procore, Sage, and the bank.
Backup and recovery
Offsite backups for Sage, M365, and any remaining on-prem servers. Tested restores quarterly — not just configured and forgotten. Documented runbook for a ransomware event.
